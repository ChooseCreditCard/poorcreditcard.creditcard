Compare Cards for Poor Credit

What counts as “poor credit”?

Poor credit typically refers to a credit score below the range accepted for most mainstream cards. Common causes include:

  • • Late or missed payments
  • • Collections or charge-offs
  • • High credit utilization
  • • Very short credit history

Best card types for poor credit

Secured credit cards

Highest approval rates. Deposit-funded limits reduce lender risk.

Low-limit unsecured cards

No deposit required. Starts small and grows with good behavior.

Credit builder programs

Hybrid products with reporting, subscriptions, and virtual cards.

Retail/store cards

Easier approval, but usually high interest and limited usability.

A simple rebuilding plan

  • • Pay every statement on time (this matters more than anything)
  • • Keep balances under 30% of your limit
  • • Don’t apply for too many cards at once
  • • Use the card every month, even in small amounts

What to expect with poor credit approvals

Issuers may offer:

  • • Lower starting limits
  • • Higher APR
  • • Fewer perks (initially)
  • • Upgrade review after 6–18 months
